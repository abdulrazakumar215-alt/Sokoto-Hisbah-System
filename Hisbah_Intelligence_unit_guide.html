Security Rules

1. Mission and Mandate

The primary mandate of the Hisbah Intelligence section is to **gather intelligence** concerning Sharia law violations and criminal activities within the state.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Illicit drug consumption and concealed sale.
  • Gatherings for fornication and hidden immorality sites (Brothels).
  • Gambling dens and other forms of financial transgressions.
  • Gathering information on false reports (Fake news) that may incite public unrest.

Special Responsibilities:

  • Operating covertly, without public knowledge of your Hisbah affiliation.
  • Submitting a complete report with evidence before taking action.
  • Protecting lives and property while gathering intelligence.

2. Personnel Qualities

The Hisbah Intelligence Officer must possess the following qualities:

Trustworthiness and Secrecy

This is the highest quality. The officer must protect all secret information obtained, and never disclose it to unauthorized persons.

Observation and Attention

The ability to notice small details, environmental changes, and public happenings without drawing attention to oneself.

Local Knowledge

Knowing the streets, neighborhoods, local customs, and how the residents conduct their affairs.

Prudent Courage

Being courageous enough to enter danger, but using wisdom to avoid confrontation or exposing yourself.

Communication Skills

Knowing how to speak with the public, extract information from people, and report clearly.

3. Essential Tools and Equipment

These are the necessary tools for conducting covert operations. Click each one to confirm you have prepared it.

1. Quality Mobile Phone (with GPS)

With a good camera, strong Password, and Encrypted Messaging Apps for communication.

2. Writing Materials and Diary

A small notebook for writing down observed data. Never write your name inside.

3. Work Clothes (Casual/Disguise)

Wear clothing that makes you blend in as an ordinary citizen without drawing attention.

4. Cover Identification (Cover ID)

If issued, it must be protected carefully at all times.

5. Powerful Battery (Power Bank)

To ensure your phone does not run out of battery during intelligence gathering.

4. Operational Procedures

This section divides operational steps into two: Field data collection, and how to use the App to send reports.

Information Gathering Process

Passive Surveillance: Remain near the suspected location, but do not show that you are observing. Act or sit like any ordinary citizen.

Triple Verification:

  1. Report: Obtaining a report from a source.
  2. Observation: Going and seeing it yourself.
  3. Evidence: Gathering evidence (photo or video) for confirmation.
Crucial Rule: 'Do Not Enter a Difficult Situation'

If you realize you might be identified or your life is in danger, **Withdraw Immediately**. Officer safety is more important than intelligence gathering during an emergency.
